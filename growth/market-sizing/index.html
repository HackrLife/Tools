Methodology · Proof of Work

The Bottom-Up Method

Top-down market sizing ("the global CRM market is $50B, we only need 1%") is a fiction that gets funded and then fails. This tool forces the opposite discipline — count the entities first, then multiply by what each one is actually worth to you.

Core Formula

Real Market Size

Your true Serviceable Addressable Market is not a percentage of some analyst report. It is the number of accounts you can actually reach, multiplied by what each account is worth annually.

Real_Market_Size = Reachable_Entities × [ACV + (ADV × 365)]
Reachable_EntitiesVerified accounts in your CRM or target database — not estimates
ACVAnnual Contract Value — fixed recurring revenue per account per year
ADV × 365Usage-based annualised revenue — zero if your model is purely subscription

Why entity count is everything. If you cannot name the entities, you cannot sell to them. Market sizing without a verified entity list is creative writing. The entity count is the ceiling on your ambition — until you expand the ICP.

Resource Formula

Hiring Logic — The Resource Gap

Hires_Required = [(Market_Size × Target_%) − Current_ARR] ÷ Quota_Per_Rep
Market_Size × Target_%Revenue at the target penetration level (15%, 30%, or 50%)
Current_ARRRevenue already captured — subtracted to get the incremental gap
Quota_Per_RepAnnual revenue target per AE — the unit of sales capacity

This formula tells you how many salespeople you need to hire to hit each milestone — assuming quota attainment and win rate stay constant. It is a floor on headcount, not a ceiling. Over-hire for coverage; under-hire and you will miss the window.

The Three Phases

Penetration Milestones

Markets are not captured linearly. Each penetration band requires a different strategy, a different motion, and a different org design.

Phase 1 — 15% Penetration
Entry Phase · Early Majority
Human-led sales is the right motion. Every deal teaches you something about the ICP. Focus on win rate improvement, not headcount growth. Calculate hires needed and lead velocity required to close this phase within the fiscal year.
Phase 2 — 30% Penetration
Scale Phase · Begin PLG Transition
Human-led CAC typically stops scaling efficiently here. Begin building product-led growth motions, automated nurture sequences, and self-serve onboarding. Do not double headcount to force growth past this point — the unit economics will deteriorate.
Phase 3 — 50% Penetration
Dominance Phase · Saturation Warning
You are approaching the ceiling of your current ICP. The tool issues a saturation warning. Recommended actions: raise ACV through pricing power, expand the ICP to adjacent segments, or launch a new product line into the existing customer base.

Condition

Glossary

All Terms Defined

SAMServiceable Addressable Market — the portion of TAM you can realistically reach with your current model
ACVAnnual Contract Value — fixed recurring revenue per customer per year
ADVAverage Daily Value — usage or consumption-based revenue per customer per day
ARRAnnual Recurring Revenue — your total contracted recurring revenue today
Win RatePercentage of qualified opportunities that convert to Closed-Won deals
QuotaAnnual revenue target assigned to a single AE or growth manager
PenetrationPercentage of reachable entities that have become paying customers
PLGProduct-Led Growth — acquisition, expansion, and retention driven by the product itself rather than by sales headcount
ICPIdeal Customer Profile — the specific firmographic and behavioural definition of your best-fit customer segment
CACCustomer Acquisition Cost — total sales and marketing spend divided by new customers acquired
